🌏 Global Industry Shifts — SEA Impact

Samsung exits China TV & appliance sales; Suzhou lines shift to export

Samsung is withdrawing from China's home appliance and TV market in 2026, citing intense competition from local brands. Production lines in Suzhou will pivot to export, with the United States and Southeast Asia as primary destinations. Samsung's semiconductor and smartphone businesses in China continue unaffected.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Increased Samsung appliance supply into SEA markets; potential pricing pressure on local distributors.
Source: Nikkei Asia, SharpPost, 27 Apr 2026

Chinese home appliance giants flock to Thailand; Midea, Hisense, TCL expand

Midea's Chonburi AC factory targets 6M units for 2026 cold year, with over 98% local staff. Hisense broke ground on HHA Intelligent Manufacturing Industrial Park in Amata Chonburi. Haier began construction of an AI-powered central AC base in Rayong with $147M investment. TCL Zhijia announced $680M factory in Chonburi.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Thailand becomes primary AC export hub for US & ASEAN markets; local component demand surges.
Source: Tiger Brokers, China Daily, 13 May 2026

China+1 goes mainstream; Vietnam, Thailand, Indonesia lead manufacturing shift

Global buyers now require at least one non-China production site as a near-prerequisite for orders. Vietnam, Thailand, and Indonesia are the primary beneficiaries. China remains essential for electronics and semiconductors, but diversification is structural.

→ Downstream SEA impact: EMS/ODM capacity expansion accelerates; component supply chains follow.
Source: Dimerco, Manhattan SEZ, May 2026

Chinese brands surge across ASEAN beyond electronics; Haier, Midea, BYD lead

Euromonitor report finds Chinese brands expanding into beauty, food service, and home appliances across ASEAN. In home appliances, Chinese AC market share rose from 9% (2015) to 25% (2024). BYD is now top EV brand in most SEA markets.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Intensified competition for local and Japanese/Korean appliance brands; supply chain localization deepens.
Source: China Daily, Euromonitor, 22 May 2026

SEA smartphone shipments decline 9% in Q1 2026; ASP hits record $349

Omdia reports 21.6M units shipped in Q1 2026, down 9% YoY. Average selling price reached $349, up 19% YoY, driven by memory cost inflation. Over 60% of SEA smartphones priced below $200 face growing affordability pressure.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Vendors prioritize profitability over volume; price-sensitive segments shrink.
Source: Omdia, 19 May 2026

📜 Policy Focus — SEA Regulatory Update

Indonesia — Revised e-commerce regulations after seller complaints

Indonesia is preparing to revise Trade Minister Regulation No. 31/2023 on electronic commerce, following complaints about rising platform fees and operational costs. Trade Minister Budi Santoso aims to finalize revisions soon, balancing platform-seller relations and consumer protection.

Status: Under discussion

Indonesia — TKDN requirements relaxed under MOI Regulation No. 35/2025

New regulation simplifies TKDN calculation methods and introduces investment-based incentives for companies investing in R&D, technology transfer, or domestic industrial development. Verification process now fully electronic via SIINas.

Status: In effect

Malaysia — New MEPS requirements for home appliances; SIRIM certification updates

2026 standard updates require air conditioners and refrigerators to pass energy efficiency testing for SIRIM certification. Wireless devices must obtain both SIRIM Safety/EMC certification and MCMC labeling. Temporary tax exemption for re-imported goods granted.

Status: In effect

Singapore — CSA mandates Cyber Trust Mark for cybersecurity service providers by end-2026

Licensed cybersecurity service providers must obtain active CTM Promoter (Tier 3) certification by 31 Dec 2026. IMDA certifications valid for 5 years; Cybersecurity Labelling Scheme (CLS) for consumer IoT devices continues.

Status: Mandatory from 2027

Vietnam — Potential Section 301 tariffs on electronics; framework agreement signed

US signed a framework agreement with Vietnam maintaining a 20% reciprocal rate, with potential 0% duty on some articles in Annex III. Section 301 investigations initiated in March 2026 could place additional tariffs on Vietnamese electronics.

⚠️ High risk — monitor Section 301 developments
