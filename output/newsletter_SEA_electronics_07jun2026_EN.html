Global Industry Shifts with SEA Impact

Smartphone ASP Hits Record High in SEA

Southeast Asia’s smartphone market declined 9% YoY in 1Q26 to 21.6 million units, but average selling price (ASP) reached a record $349, up 19% YoY. Memory cost inflation reset device pricing across the region. vivo and OPPO recorded the strongest ASP growth among major vendors at 28% and 26% respectively, reflecting a strategic shift away from low-margin entry-level shipments.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Vendors prioritise profitability over volume; entry-level segment shrinks.
Source: Omdia, May 19, 2026

Chinese Brands Dominate SEA Online 3C Categories

In Indonesia's online 3C category, Chinese brands hold 70.3% combined share of the leading brand cohort, a +1.3 percentage-point lift versus 2024. Xiaomi leads outright at 8.6% with the POCO X7 Pro as flagship. The pattern is consistent across consumer electronics categories in the region.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Chinese brands have moved from cross-border exporters to category-dominant cohorts.
Source: Moojing Global, 2026

US Reciprocal Tariffs Framework Signed with Vietnam, Malaysia, Thailand

The US administration signed framework agreements maintaining reciprocal tariff rates: Vietnam at 20%, Malaysia at 19%, Thailand at 19%, and the Philippines at 19%. Zero-tariff lists for certain articles are pending. These agreements shape the cost structure for consumer electronics exports from SEA to the US.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Tariff certainty supports continued manufacturing investment in SEA.
Source: Dimerco US Tariff Update, 2026; ASEAN Briefing, 2025

China+1 Strategy Accelerates SEA Manufacturing Shift

By 2026, Southeast Asia is a key region for consumer electronics manufacturing under the "China Plus One" strategy, with Vietnam and Indonesia leading in diversification and growth. Tariffs and geopolitical risks drive this shift. Companies must now consider tariffs, geopolitical risks, trade agreements, and supply chain resilience alongside cost and labour.

→ Downstream SEA impact: SEA seen as extension of China’s supply chain network, offering proximity and improving infrastructure.
Source: Vietnam Sourcing, 2026; ARC Group, 2026

Policy Focus

Indonesia TKDN Reform: Weighted-Factor System Introduced

Under MOI Reg. 35/2025, the TKDN for goods calculation shifts from a cost-based approach to a weighted-factor system. Raw materials/components account for up to 75% of the total TKDN weighting. The expanded TKDN regime is expected to apply not only to hardware but also AI systems and SaaS platforms. Blockchain governance for compliance is planned from 2026 onward.

Status: In effect
Compliance Alert: New weighting system changes local content calculation methodology.

Singapore & Japan Mutual Recognition for IoT Cybersecurity Labels

CSA and METI of Japan signed a Memorandum of Cooperation to mutually recognise IoT cybersecurity labelling schemes CLS and JC-STAR. The MoC took effect June 1, 2026. Singapore's Cybersecurity Labelling Scheme (CLS) for routers will increase from Level 1 to Level 2 by 2027.

Status: Effective June 1, 2026
Compliance Alert: Manufacturers can use one certification for both Singapore and Japan markets.

Malaysia MIDA Expatriate Policy Revised Effective June 2026

The MIDA Expatriate System (MES) becomes the sole platform for expatriate applications from June 1, 2026. Category I minimum salary increased to RM20,000 with a tenure limit of 10 years. Category II minimum salary increased to RM10,000–RM19,999 with a tenure limit of 10 years. Category III minimum salary increased to RM5,000–RM9,999 with a tenure limit of 5 years.

Status: Effective June 1, 2026

Malaysia Digital Service Tax Applies to Foreign Digital Service Providers

The Malaysian government amended the Service Tax Act 2018, imposing the requirement on foreign service providers to charge and collect service tax from consumers and remit it to the Malaysian tax authority for prescribed digital services rendered to Malaysian consumers.

Status: In effect
