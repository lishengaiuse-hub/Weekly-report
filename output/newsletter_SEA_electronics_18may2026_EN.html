Global Industry Shifts

Samsung Exits China Home Appliance Market; Suzhou Plant Stays Open for Exports

Samsung confirmed on 6 May 2026 it has stopped selling home appliances and TVs in mainland China, ending a 34-year presence. The Suzhou plant will continue production, pivoting entirely to export markets. The move reflects mounting pressure from local Chinese brands and a strategic shift toward semiconductors.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Samsung's Southeast Asian factories may absorb additional export volume; Suzhou output likely redirected to SEA distribution hubs.
Source: Digitimes, 7 May 2026

Haier Begins Construction of AI-Powered Commercial AC Factory in Thailand

Haier started building an AI-powered factory in Thailand, targeting regional commercial air conditioning demand. The facility will feature over 65% automation. Market forecasts indicate regional order demand for commercial AC systems will exceed 220,000 units by 2030.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Strengthens Thailand's position as a manufacturing hub for smart, energy-efficient HVAC equipment.
Source: Chinadaily.com.cn, 13 May 2026

Foxconn to Manufacture Humanoid Robots in Vietnam Starting November 2026

Fushan Technology (Vietnam), a Foxconn subsidiary, plans to install new production lines from May to August 2026, trial in September-October, and begin official production in November 2026. Foxconn has invested $4 billion in Vietnam since 2007, employing 130,000 people.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Marks a significant diversification beyond consumer electronics EMS into advanced robotics manufacturing in Vietnam.
Source: TNGlobal, 21 April 2026

Chinese Home Appliance Brands Accelerate SEA Factory Expansion to Avoid Tariffs

Major Chinese brands including Midea, Haier, and TCL are expanding production in Vietnam and Thailand as part of the "China Plus One" strategy. The shift is driven by Western tariffs and growing local demand. Industry sources note that Southeast Asia is overcrowded and short on skilled labor, with rising wages and logistics costs.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Intensifies competition for industrial land and skilled labor across Vietnam and Thailand.
Source: China Washing Machine News, 11 May 2026

Sunhouse Targets USD 120 Million in Exports; Strengthens R&D and Manufacturing

Vietnam's leading home appliance brand Sunhouse aims for USD 120 million in exports in 2026, leveraging robust R&D and large-scale manufacturing. The company showcased at the 139th Canton Fair, reinforcing its position in the global supply chain under the "China Plus One" strategy.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Highlights Vietnam's growing capability in home appliance OEM/ODM beyond simple assembly.
Source: Thailand Business News, 6 May 2026

Policy

Philippines Imposes 12% VAT on All Electronics Imports

The Philippines applies a 12% Value Added Tax (VAT) on all electronics imports, calculated on the dutiable value (CIF + customs duty). The 12% VAT applies even when customs duty is 0% under ITA or FTA agreements. PEZA-registered companies importing for export manufacturing are exempt.

VAT-registered businesses can claim import VAT as an input tax credit.

Compliance Alert

Importers must factor in 12% VAT on CIF value; PEZA registration offers exemption for export-oriented manufacturers.
