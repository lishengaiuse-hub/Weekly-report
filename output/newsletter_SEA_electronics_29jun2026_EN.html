Policy Focus

Malaysia: SIRIM Certification and LVG Sales Tax for Electronics Imports

Effective 1 May 2026, Malaysia enforces stricter import compliance for electronic, electrical, and mechanical equipment, mandating SIRIM certification and local representative registration. A 10% sales tax is imposed on cross-border direct shipments valued under MYR 500.

Status: In Effect

Malaysia: MIDA New Expatriate Policy Takes Effect June 2026

MIDA's revised expatriate policy includes increased minimum salary requirements (Category I: RM20,000, Category II: RM10,000-RM19,999, Category III: RM5,000-RM9,999) and tenure limits of 5-10 years, with replacement plans required for some categories.

Status: In Effect

Singapore: Cybersecurity Labelling Scheme for Routers to Be Upgraded to Level 2 by End-2027

Singapore's CSA and IMDA will upgrade mandatory cybersecurity standards for all residential routers from CLS Level 1 to Level 2 by end-2027, following the discovery of 2,700 infected devices in a global botnet. Level 2 requires stronger encryption, secure authentication, and firmware integrity.

Status: Announced, Effective End-2027

Indonesia: TKDN Policy Reform Under MOI Regulation No. 35/2025

Indonesia is revamping its TKDN (local content) rules to simplify compliance and reward investments. The regulation aims to balance domestic industry growth with global competitiveness, with a focus on streamlining compliance and incentivizing investment.

Status: In Effect

US Section 301 Tariffs: Vietnam, Thailand, Philippines Face Proposed 12.5% Tariff

The USTR is proposing a 12.5% Section 301 tariff on goods originating from Vietnam, Thailand, and the Philippines for failing to enforce forced labor import prohibitions. Vietnam has signed a framework agreement to potentially keep its reciprocal rate at 20% with 0% duty on some articles.

Alert: Proposed tariffs could impact consumer electronics exports from these countries to the US.

Singapore to Upgrade Router Cybersecurity Standards to Level 2 by End-2027

Singapore's Cyber Security Agency (CSA) and IMDA will upgrade mandatory cybersecurity standards for all residential routers from Cybersecurity Labelling Scheme (CLS) Level 1 to Level 2 by end-2027. This follows the discovery of 2,700 infected Singapore devices participating in a global botnet.

Level 2 certification requires stronger protections such as better encryption, secure authentication, and firmware integrity.

Compliance Alert
