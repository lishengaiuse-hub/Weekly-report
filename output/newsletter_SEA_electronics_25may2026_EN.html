🌐 Global Industry Shifts — SEA Impact

Samsung exits China home appliance market

On 6 May 2026, Samsung Electronics announced its withdrawal from the home appliance business in China, while committing to maintain after-sales service. Samsung entered China's home appliance market in 1992; its TV market share once exceeded 10% but has now fallen to less than 3%.

⬇️ Downstream SEA impact: Samsung's SEA appliance strategy may see increased focus on Vietnam and Thailand production bases.
Source: LinkedIn (Bill Jia post), 6 May 2026

Chinese brands surge across ASEAN beyond EVs and electronics

According to a Euromonitor International report, Chinese consumer brands are rapidly expanding across Southeast Asia into beauty, food service, and home appliances. The ASEAN economies of Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, and Vietnam account for 95% of the region's $4 trillion GDP.

⬇️ Downstream SEA impact: Chinese home appliance brands (Haier, Midea, TCL, Hisense) are deepening factory investments in Thailand.
Source: China Daily, 22 May 2026

US Section 301 investigation targets Vietnam electronics

The USTR is investigating Vietnam's acts, policies, and practices relating to structural excess capacity in sectors including electronic equipment, machinery, and footwear. A 25-50% tariff on Vietnamese electronics is possible by July 2026.

⬇️ Downstream SEA impact: Could accelerate supply chain rebalancing to Malaysia and Thailand.
Source: Torres Trade Law table, 7 May 2026; Carra Globe, May 2026

US reciprocal tariffs framework signed with Thailand and Philippines

Thailand's reciprocal rate set at 19%; Philippines at 17%. Both frameworks include potential 0% duty on certain articles in Annex III of Executive Order 14346.

⬇️ Downstream SEA impact: Consumer electronics exports from Thailand and Philippines to US may see partial relief.
Source: Dimerco US Tariff Update, May 2026; Torres Trade Law, 7 May 2026

🔧 Core Component Supply Chain Update

PCB/FPC: Meiko Electronics invests $50M in new Vietnam plant

Japanese PCB manufacturer Meiko Electronics will establish a new subsidiary, Meiko Electronics Yen Quang, in Vietnam to meet rising regional demand from customers advancing supply chain localization in ASEAN.

📍 Vietnam · Status: New investment announced April 2026

Sourcing Implication: Adds rigid-flex and HDI PCB capacity in Vietnam for consumer electronics assemblers.

Display: Samsung Display $1.8B OLED plant in Vietnam operational

Samsung Display's $1.8 billion Vietnamese OLED plant can process 10 million units yearly, serving as a hedge against trade friction. OLED overtook LCD as dominant smartphone panel in 2024 with 56% share.

📍 Vietnam · Status: Operational

Sourcing Implication: Secures OLED supply for SEA smartphone assembly; reduces dependency on China.

Camera Modules: LG Innotek expands Vietnam V3 plant

LG Innotek completed a new smartphone camera module plant (V3) in northern Vietnam, doubling production capacity. The company plans dual production strategy with Gumi, South Korea as mother factory and Vietnam as mass-production hub.

📍 Vietnam · Status: Operational from 2025; expansion completed

Sourcing Implication: Major Apple supplier; increased module output supports premium smartphone builds in SEA.

Battery: Highstar Power begins commercial cell production in Johor, Malaysia

Chinese lithium and sodium ion cell manufacturer Highstar Power announced commercial cell production at its 2.5GWh manufacturing base in Johor, Malaysia — the fifth battery factory to enter production in the country.

📍 Johor, Malaysia · Status: Commercial production started April 2026

Sourcing Implication: Strengthens Malaysia's position as SEA battery hub for consumer electronics and ESS.

Passive Components: MLCC supply crunch continues; Samsung mulls price increase

Murata, TDK, and Yageo are capacity-constrained in automotive-grade MLCCs. Samsung Electro-Mechanics is considering price increases following Murata's lead. New output from Murata expected no earlier than late 2026.

📍 Regional (Malaysia, Philippines, Thailand) · Status: Constrained

Sourcing Implication: Procurement teams should audit BOMs for MLCC exposure; lead times remain extended.

Compressors: Nidec Global Appliance invests $70M in Embraco compressor capacity

Nidec Global Appliance is investing $70 million to increase Embraco compressor production capacity, including $21 million for the Joinville, Brazil plant. The new Embraco Atom is the most compact variable-speed compressor for residential refrigeration.

📍 Global (Brazil plant) · Status: Investment announced

Sourcing Implication: Supports SEA home appliance OEMs (refrigerators, ACs) with energy-efficient compressor supply.

📜 Policy Focus

Indonesia: TKDN regulation relaxed under MOI Reg. 35/2025

Indonesia's Ministry of Industry introduced MOI Regulation No. 35/2025, simplifying TKDN calculation methods and introducing investment-based incentives. Companies investing in R&D, technology transfer, or domestic industrial development may receive additional TKDN value recognition. Smartphones require 35% TKDN for market access.

Status: Effective 2025; implementation ongoing in 2026

Malaysia: Budget 2026 includes accelerated capital allowances for ICT equipment

Malaysia Budget 2026 provides accelerated capital allowances (20% initial, 40% annual) on qualifying capital expenditure for ICT equipment and computer software from 11 Oct 2025 to 31 Dec 2026. MIDA is strengthening semiconductor ecosystem through talent development.

Status: Effective until 31 Dec 2026

Singapore: CSA Cybersecurity Labelling Scheme (CLS) updated

Singapore's CLS for consumer IoT devices includes four security levels. From 6 Feb 2026, only Cyber Essentials or Cyber Trustmark (2025) versions can be certified. Mutual Recognition Agreement with Germany's IT Security Label recognizes CLS(IoT) Level 2 requirements.

Status: Updated certification framework from Feb 2026

Vietnam: Under US Section 301 investigation for excess capacity

USTR is investigating Vietnam's structural excess capacity in electronic equipment, machinery, and other sectors. Potential tariffs of 25-50% on Vietnamese electronics could be imposed by July 2026.
