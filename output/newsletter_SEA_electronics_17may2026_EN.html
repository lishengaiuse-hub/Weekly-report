Global Industry Shifts

Samsung exits China home appliances, restructures SEA operations

Samsung confirmed it has stopped selling home appliances in mainland China as of 6 May 2026. The Suzhou plant remains open for export. The company is closing its home appliance factory in Malaysia and a plant in Slovakia, pivoting to a "choice and focus" strategy centred on premium products, AI, and HVAC. Samsung is consolidating Southeast Asian production, with Vietnam as a key hub.

→ SEA impact: Malaysia factory closure; Vietnam production consolidation.
Source: Seoul Economic Daily, 28 Apr 2026; Global Times, 6 May 2026; ChosunBiz, 28 Apr 2026

Chinese home appliance giants accelerate SEA factory buildout

Haier, Midea, TCL, and Hisense are expanding assembly plants in Vietnam and Thailand to bypass tariffs and serve local demand. Haier began construction of an AI-powered factory in Thailand with over 65% automation. Midea cut delivery lead times in SEA from 60 to 45 days. TCL announced a 680 million baht factory in Chonburi. Haier achieved #1 white goods sales in Vietnam and Thailand.

→ SEA impact: Increased local production capacity; shorter lead times; competitive pressure on Korean/Japanese brands.
Source: China Daily, 13 May 2026; Tiger Brokers; JET News; Midea LinkedIn

LG expands HVAC business with new Global South facility in Indonesia

LG Electronics opened a partner-operated HVAC facility in Indonesia as part of its Global South strategy. The move strengthens local production for air conditioning and heating systems in the region.

→ SEA impact: Local HVAC production in Indonesia; competitive pressure on Daikin, Panasonic.
Source: LG Global Newsroom, May 2026

Dyson accelerates new product launches in home appliance and beauty categories

Dyson launched the Hushjet Mini Cool fan (212g, 6h battery) and is accelerating new product releases in 2026 to recover post-pandemic growth. The company targets SEA as a key market for portable and beauty appliances.

→ SEA impact: New product entries in portable fan and beauty categories; competition in premium small appliances.
Source: AJU Press, 8 May 2026

Core Component Supply Chain Update

PCB/FPC: Meiko Electronics invests $50M in new Vietnam PCB plant

Japanese PCB manufacturer Meiko Electronics will invest $50 million to establish Meiko Electronics Yen Quang in Quang Ninh province, Vietnam. Construction begins April 2026, mass production slated for 2027. The plant targets 30 billion yen ($191M) sales by FY2029. The move responds to growing demand from customers and supply chain localization in ASEAN.

📍 Quang Ninh, Vietnam · Status: Construction start April 2026

Sourcing Implication: New PCB capacity in northern Vietnam; potential for shorter lead times for SEA-based EMS providers.

Display: Samsung Display showcases next-gen OLED at SID 2026; $1.8B Vietnam plant

Samsung Display is showcasing smartphone OLED panels, sensor OLEDs, and stretchable displays at Display Week 2026. The company plans a $1.8 billion OLED plant in Vietnam. Samsung and LG are widening their smartphone OLED lead over Chinese rivals BOE and CSOT, according to The Korea Herald (13 May 2026).

📍 Vietnam (planned) · Status: Investment announced

Sourcing Implication: OLED panel supply for smartphones will increasingly come from Vietnam; Korean suppliers maintain technology lead.

Camera Modules: LG Innotek doubles production capacity with new Vietnam factory

LG Innotek completed its V3 camera module plant in Hai Phong, Vietnam, doubling production capacity. The company plans to recruit 2,600 additional workers. The plant produces camera modules for smartphones and optical solutions for AR/VR and smart vehicles.

📍 Hai Phong, Vietnam · Status: Operational

Sourcing Implication: Increased supply of high-end camera modules for flagship smartphones; Vietnam solidifies role as key production base.

Battery: Sunwoda targets 2026 for mass production of all-solid-state batteries

Sunwoda plans to mass produce cost-effective all-solid-state batteries by 2026. The company has production facilities in Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Vietnam, and is expanding its global R&D and manufacturing network.

📍 Malaysia, Vietnam · Status: Ramp-up planned 2026

Sourcing Implication: Potential new battery technology for consumer electronics; SEA facilities could serve as production bases.

Passive Components: MLCC lead times extended; capacity constrained through late 2026

Murata, TDK, Yageo, and Samsung Electro-Mechanics are capacity-constrained in automotive and premium MLCC lines. Murata's capacity expansion will not deliver meaningful new output until late 2026. Samsung Electro-Mechanics has prioritized high-capacitance MLCCs for AI hardware under multi-year agreements.

📍 Global, affecting SEA assembly · Status: Constrained

Sourcing Implication: Extended lead times for MLCCs; procurement teams should audit BOMs and secure multi-year supply agreements.

Compressors: Embraco factory in India ramping up; Kulthorn remains key Thai supplier

Nidec Global Appliance's Embraco compressor factory in India is entering a crucial construction phase, with ramp-up expected by end of 2026. Kulthorn Kirby in Thailand remains a major manufacturer of reciprocating compressors for refrigeration and AC products, with sizes from 1/20 to 10 HP.

📍 India (Embraco); Thailand (Kulthorn) · Status: Under construction / Operational

Sourcing Implication: Compressor supply for home appliance manufacturing in SEA; Kulthorn continues to serve regional OEMs.

Policy Focus

Indonesia plans new e-commerce rules after seller complaints

The Indonesian government is preparing a revision of e-commerce regulations following complaints about rising platform fees and operational costs. The move aims to protect local sellers and promote domestic products. Additionally, MOI Regulation No. 35 of 2025 updates TKDN calculation methods, with new verification standards effective in 2026.

Status: Proposed revision; TKDN rules in effect 2026
Compliance Alert: Brands selling in Indonesia should reassess TKDN strategies and prepare audit-ready documentation.

Singapore raises cybersecurity requirements for residential routers to CLS Level 2 by 2027

CSA and IMDA will raise mandatory cybersecurity requirements for residential routers from CLS Level 1 to Level 2 by 2027. The Cybersecurity Labelling Scheme (CLS) rates IoT devices to help consumers make informed purchasing decisions. Certification is valid for five years.

Status: Announced 2 Mar 2026; enforcement by 2027
Compliance Alert: Router manufacturers and distributors must prepare to meet Level 2 security benchmarks for market access in Singapore.

Malaysia implements New Incentive Framework (NIF) from 1 March 2026

Malaysia's New Incentive Framework (NIF) took effect for the manufacturing sector on 1 March 2026, followed by services in Q2 2026. All new incentive applications are evaluated under NIF. Existing approvals remain unaffected. MIDA oversees implementation.

Status: Effective 1 Mar 2026 (manufacturing); Q2 2026 (services)
Strategic Read: Companies planning new manufacturing investments in Malaysia should align applications with NIF criteria.

Thailand raises import duties on consumer goods; ends de minimis exemption

Thailand's Customs Department announced a tariff realignment raising duties on imported consumer goods, including electronics accessories, toward maximum statutory ceilings (up to 30-40%). The de minimis exemption for low-value imports has ended. ICs and laptops remain at 0% under ITA.

Status: Effective 30 Mar 2026
Compliance Alert: Higher import costs for finished consumer electronics; duty-free status for components unchanged.

Philippines, Vietnam, Thailand maintain 0% import tariffs for ICs and laptops under ITA

All three countries apply 0% import duty on HS 8542 (ICs) and HS 8471 (laptops) under the Information Technology Agreement (ITA). TVs face 10% MFN in Philippines and Thailand, 15% MFN in Vietnam, with 0% under ATIGA for ASEAN-origin goods.

Status: Current
Sourcing Implication: Duty-free import of components supports regional assembly; finished goods face higher tariffs.

Indonesia's Ministry of Industry has updated the Domestic Component Level (TKDN) regulation, with new verification standards coming into effect in 2026. The new weighted formula benefits companies with strong domestic sourcing, local labour absorption, and domestic facility investment. R&D localization is now financially rewarded.

Manufacturers heavily dependent on imported components face higher compliance requirements.
