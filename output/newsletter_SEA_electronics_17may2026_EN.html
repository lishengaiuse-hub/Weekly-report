🌏 Global Industry Shifts

Samsung Exits China Home Appliances, Consolidates SEA

Samsung confirmed it has stopped selling home appliances in mainland China as of May 6, 2026. The Suzhou plant pivots to exports. The company recently decided to close its home appliance factory in Malaysia and its Slovakia plant is also scheduled to cease operations this month. Samsung is strengthening its "choice and focus" strategy, continuing to manufacture premium products like refrigerators while increasing outsourced production.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Malaysia factory closure; Vietnam becomes key production hub for Samsung appliances.
Source: BigGo Finance, ChosunBiz, Seoul Economic Daily, Global Times

Chinese Home Appliance Giants Ramp Up SEA Production

Haier, Midea, TCL, and Hisense are expanding assembly plants in Vietnam and Thailand to bypass tariffs on Chinese-made finished goods. Haier began construction of an AI-powered factory in Thailand with over 65% automation. Midea cut delivery lead times in SEA from 60 to 45 days. TCL Zhijia announced a $680 million factory in Chonburi, Thailand.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Thailand and Vietnam become primary export hubs for Chinese appliance brands to Europe, North America, and ASEAN.
Source: Tiger Brokers, China-Washingmachine.com, Chinadaily.com.cn, Midea LinkedIn

LG Expands HVAC Business with New Indonesia Facility

LG Electronics opened a new partner-operated HVAC facility in Indonesia as part of its Global South strategy. The company also hosted LG HVAC CONNECT ASIA 2026, bringing together Asian HVAC partners.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Indonesia becomes a production base for LG's HVAC products serving regional markets.
Source: LG Global Newsroom

Dyson Accelerates New Product Launches for 2026 Recovery

📜 Policy Focus

Indonesia: New E-Commerce Rules and TKDN Reform

Indonesia is preparing to revise e-commerce regulations following seller complaints about rising platform fees and operational costs. MOI Regulation No. 35 of 2025 updates the TKDN (Domestic Component Level) regime with a new weighted formula benefiting companies with strong domestic sourcing, local labour absorption, and domestic facility investment. R&D localisation is now financially rewarded. PP No. 3 of 2026 tightens direct selling and trade compliance.

Status: Revision in progress; TKDN verification standards effective 2026
Compliance Alert: Businesses should reassess TKDN strategies and prepare audit-ready documentation for the new verification standards.

Malaysia: New Incentive Framework (NIF) Effective March 2026

Malaysia's New Incentive Framework (NIF) is implemented on a phased basis starting with the manufacturing sector from March 1, 2026. All incentive applications for manufacturing received by MIDA from this date will be evaluated under NIF. Existing approvals are not affected. MCMC also announced deemed registration for large internet messaging and social media service providers with at least 8 million users.

Status: Effective for manufacturing sector since March 1, 2026; services sector Q2 2026

Singapore: Cybersecurity Labelling for Routers Raised to Level 2 by 2027

CSA and IMDA will raise mandatory cybersecurity requirements for residential routers from CLS Level 1 to Level 2 by 2027. The Cybersecurity Labelling Scheme (CLS), introduced in 2020, rates IoT device cybersecurity levels. Certification is valid for five years, requiring recertification.

Status: Announced March 2, 2026; enforcement by 2027
Compliance Alert: Manufacturers and distributors of residential routers must prepare to meet Level 2 security benchmarks to maintain market access in Singapore.

Thailand: Customs Duty Reform – Tariff Hikes on Consumer Goods

Thailand announced a tariff realignment raising duties on a wide range of imported consumer goods toward maximum statutory ceilings. Sensitive categories such as plastics, household items, and electronics accessories could see duties climb to 30% or even 40%. The de minimis threshold for low-value imports is also being eliminated.

Status: Announced March 30, 2026; implementation ongoing
Compliance Alert: Importers of consumer electronics accessories into Thailand should review duty exposure and consider local assembly options.

Philippines, Vietnam, Thailand: Import Tariffs for Electronics

Philippines: 0% for ICs and laptops (ITA), 10% MFN / 0% ATIGA for TVs, 12% VAT. Vietnam: 0% for ICs and laptops (ITA), 15% MFN / 0% ATIGA for TVs, 10% VAT. Thailand: 0% for ICs and laptops (ITA), 10% MFN / 0% ATIGA for TVs, 7% VAT. All three countries are part of RCEP, ATIGA, and ITA networks.
