Global Industry Shifts

Samsung is the only top-5 brand growing in SEA

Samsung shipped 4.6 million units in Q1 2026, up 4% YoY, capturing 21% market share. OPPO, Xiaomi, TRANSSION, and vivo all declined YoY. The overall SEA market contracted 9% to 21.6 million units, but ASP rose 19% to $349, driven by memory cost inflation.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Vendors prioritise profitability over volume; mid-range and budget segments face pricing pressure.
Source: Omdia, 19 May 2026

Chinese brands surge across ASEAN beyond electronics

Chinese consumer brands are expanding aggressively into ASEAN markets across multiple categories, including EVs, electronics, and F&B. This broadens the competitive landscape for traditional CE players.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Increased shelf competition; local distributors may see portfolio shifts.
Source: China Daily, 22 May 2026

Samsung exits China TV market; Korean brands under pressure

Samsung Electronics is retreating from China's TV and home appliance market, signalling intensifying competition from Chinese brands. The move may free up resources for SEA markets.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Potential reallocation of Samsung marketing and channel investment to SEA.
Source: DIGITIMES Asia, 22 May 2026

Core Component Supply Chain Update

PCB/FPC: Qdos Tech (Malaysia) remains key FPC supplier

Qdos Tech, a Penang-based subsidiary of Ruihua Group, continues to offer FPC design, prototyping, and mass production for consumer electronics. Malaysia's PCB ecosystem is expanding with new IC substrate capacity.

📍 Penang, Malaysia · Status: Operational

Sourcing Implication: Malaysian FPC suppliers offer a viable China+1 option for mid-volume consumer device programs.

Display: Samsung Display to build $1.8B OLED plant in Vietnam

Samsung Display is proceeding with a $1.8 billion OLED manufacturing facility in Vietnam. The plant will consolidate AMOLED module production, replacing the Tianjin, China line. OLED monitor panel shipments are projected at 5.4 million units in 2026.

📍 Vietnam · Status: Planned

Sourcing Implication: Vietnam becomes a critical hub for smartphone OLED module assembly; panel buyers should monitor ramp timelines.

Camera: LG Innotek completes V3 plant expansion in Hai Phong

LG Innotek has finished construction of its V3 camera module plant in Hai Phong, Vietnam, enabling a dual-hub strategy with Gumi, Korea. The expansion targets cost competitiveness for standard smartphone camera modules.

📍 Hai Phong, Vietnam · Status: Operational

Sourcing Implication: Increased module supply for flagship and mid-range smartphones; OEMs gain a larger non-China camera module source.

Battery: Sunwoda plans $280M consumer Li-ion factory in Vietnam

Sunwoda announced a 2 billion yuan (~$280M) investment for a consumer lithium battery factory in Vietnam. ATL (Amperex Technology) already has production facilities in the region. EVE Energy is investing in Malaysia.

📍 Vietnam · Status: Planned

Sourcing Implication: Battery cell supply for smartphones and wearables is diversifying into SEA; qualification cycles for new vendors are 12-18 months.

Passive: Murata hikes MLCC prices 15-35% from April 2026

Murata announced price increases of 15%–35% effective 1 April 2026, covering AI server, automotive, and RF MLCCs. Yageo also reports strong Q1 2026 orders driven by AI. Silver, copper, and tin prices continue to rise.

📍 Global · Status: Price increase in effect

Sourcing Implication: BOM costs for SEA-assembled devices will rise; procurement teams should secure allocation for high-cap MLCCs.

Compressors/Motors: Nidec completes Embraco acquisition; Kulthorn Kirby active in Thailand

Nidec has completed its acquisition of Embraco, expanding into refrigeration compressors. Kulthorn Kirby in Thailand manufactures compressor motors for refrigerators and ACs, using R290, R600a, and CO2 refrigerants.

📍 Thailand · Status: Operational

Sourcing Implication: Home appliance OEMs in Thailand have access to localised compressor supply for energy-efficient products.

Policy Focus

Singapore: CSA and Japan METI sign mutual recognition for IoT cybersecurity

Effective 1 June 2026, Singapore's Cybersecurity Labelling Scheme (CLS) and Japan's JC-STAR are mutually recognised. This covers IoT consumer devices. Router cybersecurity requirements are being elevated, with Level 2 mandatory from 2027.

Effective: 1 June 2026
Compliance Alert: CE manufacturers selling routers or smart home devices in Singapore must align with CLS Level 2 by 2027.

Indonesia: Revamped TKDN rule simplifies compliance with weighted-factor system

MOI Reg. 35/2025 replaces the cost-based TKDN calculation with a weighted-factor system. Raw materials/components account for up to 75% of the TKDN weighting. The policy aims to boost domestic industry while streamlining market access for electronics.

In effect: 2025/2026
Compliance Alert: CE brands must re-evaluate local sourcing strategies to meet TKDN thresholds for government procurement and e-commerce.

Malaysia: Temporary import duty relief for re-imported goods until Dec 2026

The Royal Malaysian Customs Department (RMCD) has granted a temporary exemption from import duty and sales tax for goods re-imported by their original Malaysian manufacturer due to the Middle East conflict. Effective until 31 December 2026.

Effective: Until 31 Dec 2026

Vietnam: US reciprocal tariff rate set at 20% under framework agreement

The US Administration signed a framework agreement maintaining Vietnam's reciprocal tariff rate at 20%, with potential 0% duty on certain articles. The Philippines and Thailand face proposed Section 301 rates of 12.5% (announced 2 June 2026).

Proposed: 2 June 2026
Compliance Alert: CE exporters from Vietnam to the US should monitor Annex III for potential duty-free lines; Philippine and Thai exporters face new 301 risk.

Malaysia

BREAKING
Honor

Honor Magic V6 foldable launches in Malaysia at MYR 7,699

The Honor Magic V6 debuted in Malaysia on 4 June 2026, starting at MYR 7,699 (~USD 1,900). Pre-orders ran from 4-11 June with freebies worth up to MYR 3,797. Available in Red, Gold, White, and Black.

The launch was preceded by a publicity stunt where the Magic V6 towed an aircraft, setting an Asia Record for foldable durability.

Brand Watch

Honor is aggressively positioning the Magic V6 as a premium, durable foldable. The MYR 7,699 price point undercuts Samsung's foldable range, putting pressure on the Z Fold 8 Ultra launch expected in late July.

4 Jun 2026 · GadgetMatch · source
Market Data

Malaysia smartphone pricing: Ultra-premium flagships now RM 6,000–RM 10,000

2026 pricing trends in Malaysia show ultra-premium flagships (Galaxy S26 Ultra, iPhone 17 Pro Max, Z Fold 6 Ultra) ranging from RM 6,000 to RM 10,000, up ~10% vs 2025. Standard flagships are RM 4,000–RM 6,000, up ~5%. Budget segment starts at RM 350 (Infinix Hot 50i, Samsung Galaxy A06).

Key insight: Below RM 450, expect MediaTek chipsets, no IP rating, and 720p displays. Avoid RM 299 listings — likely refurbished or unbranded clones.

Market Signal

Inflation and memory shortages are pushing entry-level pricing upward; the RM 350-799 band is shrinking. Brands must differentiate on battery and display specs at the low end.

Jun 2026 · GadgetSpecs.my / Alibaba · source
Policy

MIDA Expatriate System (MES) fully implemented from 1 June 2026

All new and renewal expatriate-related applications under MIDA's purview must now be submitted via MES through the Xpats Gateway. This affects foreign technical staff in electronics manufacturing.

Separately, MIDA continues to strengthen local supplier ecosystems in the semiconductor industry, in partnership with Daikin Malaysia and SHRDC.

Compliance Alert

EMS companies in Malaysia must ensure all expat applications use the new MES system; delays in transition may affect production line staffing.

1 Jun 2026 · MIDA · source
EMS

Inari Amertron Q3 FY2026: Revenue MYR 265.73M, net income down 50% YoY

Inari Amertron reported Q3 FY2026 (ended 31 Mar 2026) sales of MYR 265.73 million, down from MYR 308.25 million a year ago. Net income was MYR 27.98 million, halved from MYR 55.48 million. EPS fell to MYR 0.0074.

The company's revenue decline reflects weaker demand in back-end testing services for RF and optoelectronics.

Sourcing Implication

Inari's results signal softening in the SEA semiconductor test segment; EMS buyers should expect tighter capacity allocation from OSAT providers.

25 May 2026 · MarketScreener · source

Policy

CSA and Japan METI sign mutual recognition for IoT cybersecurity labelling

Singapore's Cyber Security Agency (CSA) and Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI) signed a Memorandum of Cooperation on 18 March 2026, effective 1 June 2026. The mutual recognition covers the Cybersecurity Labelling Scheme (CLS) and JC-STAR.

Router cybersecurity requirements are being elevated, with Level 2 mandatory from 2027.

Compliance Alert

IoT device manufacturers can now use a single certification for both Singapore and Japan markets. This reduces time-to-market for smart home products.

18 Mar 2026 · Approve IT / CSA · source
Market Data

Singapore home appliance market: Premium, smart-feature focused

Singapore, along with Malaysia and Thailand, is classified under Playbook 2 for SEA home appliances: premium positioning, smart-feature category leadership, and mid-to-high-end pricing. This contrasts with the small-appliance, sub-CNY 1,000 playbook for Vietnam, Indonesia, and the Philippines.

Key players include Philips, Whirlpool, Electrolux, and Bosch.

Strategic Read

Brands entering Singapore must prioritise smart connectivity and energy efficiency over low price points. The market rewards innovation and brand premium.

Smartphones · TKDN · Home Appliances
Market Data

Indonesia home appliance market grows 43.8% YoY, fastest in SEA

Indonesia's home appliance market recorded 43.8% YoY growth, the fastest in the region. Growth is broad-based across categories. The market is driven by rising online penetration and promotional events like Singles' Day and double-date promotions.

Indonesia is classified under Playbook 1: small-appliance, sub-CNY 1,000, multi-channel.

Market Signal

High growth in small appliances creates opportunities for Chinese and local brands. E-commerce channel strategy is critical for capturing impulse purchases.

2026 · NIQ / Moojing Global · source
Policy

Indonesia revamps TKDN with weighted-factor system; new e-commerce rules planned

MOI Reg. 35/2025 replaces the cost-based TKDN calculation with a weighted-factor system. Raw materials/components account for up to 75% of the TKDN weighting. Indonesia is also planning new e-commerce rules after seller complaints, aiming to boost SMEs.

TKDN is mandatory for government procurement and increasingly relevant for e-commerce platforms.

Compliance Alert

CE brands must map their component sourcing to the new weighting system. Non-compliance risks exclusion from government tenders and major e-commerce channels.

2026 · ARC Group / ANTARA News · source
Market Data

SEA smartphone Q1 2026: Samsung leads Indonesia with 21% share

Omdia data shows Samsung leading SEA with 21% market share in Q1 2026, shipping 4.6 million units. OPPO (20%), Xiaomi (17%), TRANSSION (16%), and vivo (9%) follow. Indonesia is the largest single market in the region.

Overall SEA shipments declined 9% YoY to 21.6 million units, but ASP rose 19% to $349.

Market Signal

Indonesia's smartphone market is value-driven; the ASP increase may slow volume growth in the price-sensitive segment. Brands must balance feature upgrades with affordability.

19 May 2026 · Omdia · source

BREAKING
EMS

Foxconn subsidiary Shunsin to start IC trial production in June 2026

Shunsin Technology, a Foxconn subsidiary, plans to invest $80 million in northern Vietnam to produce integrated circuits. Trial production is scheduled for June 2026, with official operation from December 2026. Annual output is 4.5 million items, all for export to the US, EU, and Japan.

Labour demand is 1,450 people, including 35 foreigners.

Sourcing Implication

Vietnam is moving up the value chain from assembly to IC fabrication. This project signals growing semiconductor backend capability in the north.

EMS

Luxshare-ICT invests additional $209M in central Vietnam; phases reach full ops by June 2026

Luxshare-ICT is investing an additional $209 million in Nghe An province, Vietnam. The second, third, and fourth phases are set to reach official operation from September 2025, June 2026, and June 2026 respectively. Total investment in Nghe An now exceeds $1.1 billion.

The expansion will create 25,000 jobs and boost annual production capacity.

Sourcing Implication

Luxshare's ramp in central Vietnam adds significant EMS capacity for Apple and other OEMs, reducing reliance on northern Vietnam and China.

BYD Vietnam plans full operations at Phu Tho plant by June 2026

BYD Vietnam expects to complete regulatory procedures and construct new components at its Phu Tho plant between July 2025 and February 2026, with full operations by June 2026. The factory currently employs 11,000 workers with estimated revenue of $2.8 billion.

BYD is also building a power battery plant in Vietnam as part of its global production network.

Sourcing Implication

BYD's Vietnam expansion strengthens the country's electronics and battery manufacturing ecosystem, offering OEMs a large-scale, vertically integrated partner.

Policy

US proposes 12.5% Section 301 tariff on Philippines

The US Administration announced a proposed 12.5% Section 301 tariff on the Philippines on 2 June 2026, citing forced labour concerns. Exemptions apply for items subject to Section 232 tariffs and items listed in Annex A. The tariff is threatened, not yet final.

Compliance Alert

CE exporters from the Philippines to the US should monitor the Federal Register notice and prepare for potential cost increases. Diversification of export markets may be necessary.
