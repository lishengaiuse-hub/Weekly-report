🌍 Global Industry Shifts — SEA Impact

MLCC Price Surge Cycle Begins

Spot prices for MLCCs have risen 15-20% since late February 2026, with high-capacity AI server models up 50-60%. Murata and Samsung Electro-Mechanics issued price increase notices to customers. Yageo signals potential further hikes in 2H26.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Higher BOM costs for smartphone and appliance assemblers in Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia.
Source: 36Kr, X (Jukan), 7Setronic

Southeast Asia's consumer electronics export sector valued at over $145B, growing at 7.2% CAGR through 2030. Vietnam, Indonesia, and Malaysia are primary beneficiaries of manufacturing diversification from China.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Increased component sourcing from local EMS providers; capacity expansion across PCB, battery, and assembly segments.
Source: Alibaba.com Seller Blog, Z2Data, Acclime

Chinese Brands Challenge Japanese/Korean Dominance in SEA

Chinese consumer brands are accelerating expansion in Southeast Asia through acquisitions and online channels, pushing into home appliance and cosmetics markets once dominated by Japanese, South Korean, and European companies.

→ Downstream SEA impact: Pricing pressure on legacy brands; increased competition for distribution and shelf space in Indonesia, Thailand, and Vietnam.
Source: KrASIA, Fortune

Enacted 8 June 2026, replacing Reg. 31/2023. Expands platform obligations to actively oversee merchant activities, pricing practices, and product traceability. Serious penalties for non-compliance. Platforms have 18 months to comply.

Effective: 8 June 2026 · Compliance deadline: ~Dec 2027

Effective 1 May 2026, mandatory SIRIM certification and local representative registration for electronic, electrical, and mechanical equipment imports. 10% sales tax on cross-border direct shipments valued under MYR 500.

Effective: 1 May 2026
⚠️ Compliance Alert: Exporters must secure SIRIM certification and appoint local representatives before shipping.

Policy

Indonesia Enacts New E-Commerce Regulation (Trade Reg. 19/2026) — Platform Gatekeeping

On 8 June 2026, the Minister of Trade enacted Regulation No. 19 of 2026 on Electronic Commerce, replacing Reg. 31/2023. The new regulation significantly expands e-commerce platform obligations to actively oversee merchant activities, pricing practices, consumer-facing information, and product traceability. Serious penalties for non-compliance. Platforms have 18 months to comply.

The regulation applies to all e-commerce platforms, both domestic and foreign, operating in Indonesia.

Compliance Alert

Electronics brands selling via e-commerce in Indonesia must ensure platform partners implement merchant monitoring and pricing oversight systems.

8 Jun 2026 · Rajah & Tann Asia · source

Indonesia Revamps TKDN Rules: Simplifying Compliance and Rewarding Investments

Indonesia's TKDN (local content) regulatory framework continues evolving. MOI Regulation No. 35/2025 introduces updated TKDN and BMP thresholds. Government and SOE procurement policies expected to be revised. Increased audits and verification processes anticipated in 2026.

Companies must proactively review supplier certifications, realign local-content targets, and maintain traceable documentation.

Compliance Alert

Electronics manufacturers must ensure TKDN compliance for government procurement eligibility; early preparation recommended.
